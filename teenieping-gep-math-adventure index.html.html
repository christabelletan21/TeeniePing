Why does math matter to Timesping?

×

Timesping: "Every morning I pick apples with my friends! Instead of counting one‑by‑one, I count in groups — that's multiplying!"

Timesping picks 3 rows of 4 apples. That's 3 × 4:

🍎🍎🍎🍎
🍎🍎🍎🍎
🍎🍎🍎🍎

3 × 4 = 12 apples!

Bigger numbers, same idea: 23 × 4

Split 23 = 20 + 3
Tens 20 × 4 = 80
Ones 3 × 4 = 12
Add 80 + 12 = 92

23 × 4 = 92!

÷

Shareping: "Later, I share my 12 apples fairly with 3 friends. That's dividing — the opposite of multiplying!"

12 apples shared into 3 equal baskets is 12 ÷ 3:

🧺🍎🍎🍎🍎
🧺🍎🍎🍎🍎
🧺🍎🍎🍎🍎

12 ÷ 3 = 4 apples each!

Bigger numbers, same idea: 96 ÷ 4

Split 96 = 80 + 16
Tens 80 ÷ 4 = 20
Ones 16 ÷ 4 = 4
Add 20 + 4 = 24

96 ÷ 4 = 24!

For Parents & Teachers

Keep the learning going offline

  • Use snacks, toy blocks or stairs to make groups — real objects make multiplication click faster than worksheets alone.
  • Turn walks into skip‑counting games: count by 2s, 5s or 10s together out loud.
  • Frame division as fair sharing: "If 3 friends split these grapes evenly, how many does each get?"
  • Celebrate effort and strategy, not just correct answers — it keeps kids willing to try tricky ones.
  • Keep sessions short. Ten focused minutes beats an hour of frustration.
